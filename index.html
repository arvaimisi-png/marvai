Referencia #1: Szelepfurat pérs CASE STUDY #1: RIM VALVE HOLE PRESS

Kiemelt műszaki kompetenciák: Core Technical Domains: hibabiztos biztonsági logika Dinamikus, kamerás (gépi látás alapú) zárt hurkú robotpozicionálás, hidraulikus préselés, biztonsági PLC Dynamic Robot-Vision Closed-Loop Alignment, Hydraulic Pressing, Fail-Safe Safety Logic

Context & Operational Problem:

Nagy pontosságú autóipari gyártócellában szükséges volt a felnik szelepfuratainak automatikus beállítása és lukasztása. A betöltő konvejorról érkező felnik szögeltérése elérte a ±15°-ot.

High-precision automotive manufacturing cell required automated alignment and punching of valve holes on wheel rims fed from a loading conveyor with high angular orientation variance (up to ±15°).

System Architecture & Scope of Ownership
  • • Hardware Topology: 2x FANUC R-2000iB/165F Industrial Robots | Omron FH Vision System | Siemens G120 VFD Hydraulic Power Units
  • • Software / Controllers: Siemens Fail-Safe PLC & HMI programmed via TIA Portal | FANUC KAREL/TP Motion Logic | PROFINET Fieldbus
  • • Personal Engineering Role: Lead Controls Engineer — Owned complete fail-safe PLC/HMI software, FANUC robot motion logic, vision system integration, and hydraulic VFD control parameters.

Key Technical Challenge & Engineering Solution: Closed-Loop Vision-to-Robot Alignment without Mechanical Fixtures

A beérkező felnik akár ±15°-os szögeltérést mutattak. A drága mechanikus központosító egységek elkerülése és a cella alapterületének csökkentése érdekében dinamikus robot-vision zárt hurkú igazítási algoritmust fejlesztettem ki:

Incoming rims introduced up to ±15° of angular misalignment on the conveyor. To avoid expensive mechanical re-centering mechanisms and reduce cell footprint, a dynamic vision-to-robot closed-loop alignment algorithm was engineered:

[Camera Image Capture]  --->  [PLC Offset Vector Calculation]  --->  [Dynamic TCP Shift]  --->  [Robot Realignment]

A robot a felnit egy fix referencia-ponthoz viszi az Omron FH kamera elé. A PLC kiszámítja a szögeltérést, menet közben frissíti a robot TCP-jét (Tool Center Point), majd behelyezi a felnit a présbe.

The robot presents the rim to the Omron FH camera at a fixed reference point. The PLC calculates the angular error offset from the vision data, updates the robot's Tool Center Point (TCP) on the fly, and re-verifies position before inserting the rim into the press tool.

Quantified Engineering Outcomes & Impact
  • ✔ Eliminated Mechanical Fixtures: Saved ~20% cell footprint and removed high-wear mechanical centering hardware.
  • ✔ Zero Scrap Rate: Fail-Safe software validation prevented off-center punching, achieving a 0% scrap rate due to mis-stamping.
  • ✔ Sub-1.5s Alignment Cycle: Executed image processing and dynamic TCP shift well within the cell's target takt time.

CASE STUDY #2: RETROFIT ECCENTRIC STAMPING PRESS

Core Technical Domains: High-Speed Motion Dynamics, Servo Positioning, Crankshaft Phase Synchronization

Context & Operational Problem:

Elektromos motorok rotor és stator lemezeinek stancolására szolgáló Schuler excenter prés modernizálása. A korábbi mechanikus rendszernél minden termékváltásnál manuális fogaskerék-cserére volt szükség.

Modernization of an industrial Schuler eccentric press used to punch rotor and stator lamination slots for electric motors. The legacy machine required manual mechanical gear replacements for every product changeover.

System Architecture & Scope of Ownership
  • • Hardware Topology: High-Speed Siemens S210 Servo Positioning Drive & Motor | Siemens G120 VFD Flywheel Drive | Rotary Encoder
  • • Software / Controllers: Siemens Fail-Safe PLC & HMI (TIA Portal) | Servo Technology Objects | High-Speed Proximity Trigger Interlocks
  • • Personal Engineering Role: Lead Software & Motion Engineer — Programmed PLC, Safety, and HMI applications; parameterized and fine-tuned servo dynamic profiles and position triggers.

Key Technical Challenge & Engineering Solution: Time-Critical Motion Control at 5 Strokes / Second (200 ms Total Cycle Window)

Másodpercenkénti 5 stancolási ciklusnál mindössze 50–70 ms állt rendelkezésre a munkadarab következő pozícióba léptetésére. Ezt a szervó gyorsulási/lassulási S-görbéinek optimális beállításával és a főtengely jeladó szinkronizációjával értem el.

With the press cycling continuously at 5 punches per second, the servo positioner had a window of only ~50–70 ms between strokes to index the workpiece to the next slot count. Achieved lock-in without mechanical bounce or collision by optimizing servo acceleration/jerk S-curves and synchronizing motion triggers directly with crankshaft encoder position.

Quantified Engineering Outcomes & Impact
  • ✔ 95% Setup Time Reduction: Cut product changeover from 45+ minutes (manual gear swap) to under 2 minutes (HMI recipe select).
  • ✔ Maintained Throughput: Achieved full production speed without mechanical collision or loss of positioning accuracy.
  • ✔ Flexible Production: Enabled arbitrary slot count programming directly from the HMI screen.

CASE STUDY #3: 20,000-METRIC-TON HEAVY HYDRAULIC PRESS OVERHAUL

Core Technical Domains: Heavy Hydraulic Safety, High-Mass Motion Control, Emergency Recovery Systems

Context & Operational Problem:

Egy 3 emelet magas, 20 000 tonnás hidraulikus prés elektromos és hidraulikus vezérlésének komplett újjáépítése egy súlyos hidraulikus meghibásodást követően, ahol a felső szerszám váratlanul lezuhant.

Complete electrical and hydraulic control restoration of a 3-story, 20,000-metric-ton press following a catastrophic hydraulic failure where the top upper mold dropped unexpectedly.

System Architecture & Scope of Ownership
  • • Hardware Topology: 3-Story Hydraulic Press Structure | High-Pressure Proportional Valve Blocks | Dual Redundant Linear Encoders | Hydraulic Pressure Feedback Transducers
  • • Software / Controllers: Omron Sysmac Studio PLC & HMI | Custom Safety State Machine | Proportional Hydraulic Pressure Ramping
  • • Personal Engineering Role: Controls Engineer — Re-architected and rewrote the complete PLC/HMI software codebase, proportional valve controls, and safety execution sequences.

Key Technical Challenge & Engineering Solution: Safety-Critical Overhaul and Free-Fall Prevention on Extreme-Mass Machinery

Rendkívül nagy tömegű szerkezet biztonságos mozgatása. Fejlett redundáns szelep-ellenőrzési rutinokat, proporcionális nyomásfelfutási profilokat és fizikai reteszelő csapokat terveztem a szabadon esés teljes elkerülésére.

Managing severe safety risks and heavy structural mass dynamics. Designed fail-safe redundant valve monitoring routines, smooth proportional pressure-ramp profiles, and physical locking pin interlocks to guarantee the multi-ton upper mold could never free-fall during power loss or pressure drop.

Quantified Engineering Outcomes & Impact
  • ✔ 100% Operational & Safety Restoration: Re-commissioned a critical manufacturing asset with zero safety compromise.
  • ✔ Eliminated Pressure Spikes: Proportional valve ramping eliminated structural shock pressure spikes, extending seal and valve life.

CASE STUDY #4: RIM MOUNTING HOLE PRESS

Core Technical Domains: Precision Servo/Hydraulic Control, Quality Capabilities (Cpk Optimization), Vision Quality Gate

Context & Operational Problem:

Automata hidraulikus prés Cella, amely a kerékpánt rögzítőfuratait préseli pontos 0.5–5% kompressziós toleranciával, egyidejűleg ellenőrizve a préselési nyomvonal geometriáját.

Automated hydraulic press cell designed to compress wheel rim mounting holes by a precise 0.5–5% compression tolerance while inspecting press mark geometry.

System Architecture & Scope of Ownership
  • • Hardware Topology: 2x 120-Ton Hydraulic Presses with Servo Rotary Chucks | FANUC R-2000iB/165F Robot | Omron FH Vision System | Siemens V90 Servo Drives
  • • Software / Controllers: Siemens Fail-Safe PLC & HMI (TIA Portal) | Omron FH Vision Inspection Software | Closed-Loop Force/Displacement Control
  • • Personal Engineering Role: Controls Engineer — Programmed Siemens PLC/HMI in TIA Portal, setup V90 servo drive dynamics, and co-developed Omron FH vision inspection logic.

Key Technical Challenge & Engineering Solution: Closed-Loop Force & Displacement Control to Achieve Cpk >= 1.67

Szigorú autóipari minőségi elvárások teljesítése változó keménységű alapanyagok mellett. Zárt hurkú erő- és elmozdulás-szabályozást valósítottam meg a kívánt Cpk érték eléréséhez.

Achieving tight automotive quality metrics across variable material hardness grades. Combined high-resolution servo rotary chuck feedback with dynamic proportional valve profiling to halt pressing at exact compression percentages (0.5–5%) across varying metal hardness levels.

Quantified Engineering Outcomes & Impact
  • ✔ High Process Capability: Consistently achieved process capability metrics of Cpk >= 1.67 across high-volume production.
  • ✔ Automated Quality Gate: Integrated inline vision inspection, eliminating downstream manual quality checks.
  • ✔ Reduced Scrap: Dynamic depth compensation reduced out-of-spec mounting hole compression to zero.

CASE STUDY #5: FORGING PRESS FLASH CUTTER

Core Technical Domains: Cross-Platform PROFIsafe Safety Integration, Inter-PLC Communication, Heavy Forging Automation

Context & Operational Problem:

Izzó melegkovácsolt darabok sorjázása és selejtkezelése nehézipari kovácsprés cellában, kettős cserélhető gyűjtőkonténeres rendszerrel.

Automated trimming and handling of red-hot forging flash within a heavy forging press cell using dual swap-out collection containers.

System Architecture & Scope of Ownership
  • • Hardware Topology: Hydraulic Flash Cutting Shear | Omron MX2 VFD | Dual Swap-Out Container System | Forging Robot
  • • Software / Controllers: Master Controller: Siemens S7-300 (SIMATIC Manager) | Sub-System: Siemens S7-1200 (TIA Portal) | PROFIsafe over PROFINET
  • • Personal Engineering Role: Electrical & Controls Systems Engineer — Designed electrical schematics in WSCAD, sourced hardware, programmed S7-1200 PLC/HMI/Safety, and established cross-platform safety signals.

Key Technical Challenge & Engineering Solution: Cross-Platform PROFIsafe Safety Coupling Between S7-300 and S7-1200

Biztonsági kapcsolat létesítése a meglévő S7-300 (SIMATIC Manager) és az új S7-1200 (TIA Portal) vezérlő között determinisztikus PROFIsafe over PROFINET kommunikációval.

Bridging safety controls between a master S7-300 (programmed in legacy SIMATIC Manager by a third party) and a new S7-1200 (programmed in TIA Portal). Configured deterministic PROFIsafe over PROFINET coupling to seamlessly share emergency stops, safety gates, and cell state machines across both software platforms.

Quantified Engineering Outcomes & Impact
  • ✔ Continuous Forging Operation: Enabled dual-container swapping without stopping the main forging press.
  • ✔ Complete Safety Isolation: Removed operator exposure to red-hot metal waste in the hazardous flash-trimming zone.
  • ✔ Seamless Integration: Delivered flawless inter-PLC safety signals across two distinct Siemens software environments.

CASE STUDY #6: SYNTHETIC FLOORING RESPOOLING ADJUSTER

Core Technical Domains: Dynamic Web Tension Control, Variable Diameter PID Optimization, Multi-VFD Synchronization

Context & Operational Problem:

Nagykapacitású áttekercselő gép, amely 4 méter széles műanyag padlóburkolatot tekercsel át nagy master tekercsekről (500–800 m) kereskedelmi kiszerelésre (15–30 m).

High-capacity rewinding machine designed to spool 4-meter-wide synthetic flooring from master rolls (500–800 m) down to commercial packaging rolls (15–30 m).

System Architecture & Scope of Ownership
  • • Hardware Topology: Master Cart Shaft Drive VFD | Core Shaft Drive VFD | Rubber-Coated Tension Drive VFD | Encoder Feedback
  • • Software / Controllers: Siemens Fail-Safe PLC & HMI (TIA Portal) | Closed-Loop PID Control Loops | Real-Time Inertia & Diameter Calculations
  • • Personal Engineering Role: Controls Programmer — Developed complete PLC/HMI application, parameterized drive groups, and tuned closed-loop tension control loops.

Key Technical Challenge & Engineering Solution: Dynamic Tension Compensation Across a 1:20 Roll Diameter Change

Egyenletes anyagfeszítés biztosítása 1:20 átmérőváltozás mellett, ráncolódás és nyúlás nélkül. Real-time átmérő- és tehetetlenség-becslő PID algoritmusokat implementáltam a PLC-ben.

Maintaining uniform web tension across a 1:20 roll diameter variation without stretching or wrinkling the material. Built active PID control loops with real-time diameter and inertia estimation inside the PLC to continuously adjust drive motor torque and speeds.

Quantified Engineering Outcomes & Impact
  • ✔ Zero Material Distortion: Completely eliminated surface stretching, wrinkling, or material deformation during spooling.
  • ✔ High Precision Cut Lengths: Delivered exact consumer roll lengths within a tight ±0.2% tolerance window.
  • ✔ Smooth Dynamic Acceleration: Programmed inertia-compensated ramping curves to prevent web breakage on start/stop.

CASE STUDY #10: HARPOON CONVEYOR SWARF MANAGEMENT SYSTEM

Core Technical Domains: Distributed Inter-PLC Safety Interlocking, Energy-Optimized Hydraulics, WSCAD Schematic Design

Context & Operational Problem:

Automata fémforgács-elszállító rendszer megmunkáló csarnokban, két mellék-szigonyos konvejorral és egy fő elszállító vonallal, forgácsdarálóval.

Automated metal chip (swarf) evacuation system in a machining hall, consisting of two sub-harpoon conveyors feeding a main harpoon discharge line and chip shredder.

System Architecture & Scope of Ownership
  • • Hardware Topology: Reciprocating Hydraulic Pistons | Siemens V20 VFD Hydraulic Pump Drives | Main & Sub Harpoon Mechanics
  • • Software / Controllers: Distributed Siemens PLCs (TIA Portal) over PROFINET | WSCAD Electrical Schematics | Idling Pump Speed Logic
  • • Personal Engineering Role: Electrical & Controls Designer — Designed electrical schematics in WSCAD, managed hardware sourcing, programmed PLC/HMI/Safety software, and tuned VFD profiles.

Key Technical Challenge & Engineering Solution: Energy Optimization and Inter-PLC Safety Interlocking

A hidraulikaolaj túlmelegedésének megakadályozása és PLC-k közötti biztonsági reteszelések kiépítése. A szivattyúk fordulatszámának csökkentése az irányváltások alatt ~30% energiamegtakarítást eredményezett.

Preventing hydraulic fluid overheating and managing inter-PLC safety interlocking. Configured pump VFDs to automatically reduce speed during piston direction reversals, and implemented PROFINET interlocks ensuring sub-conveyors operate strictly when the main conveyor is running.

Quantified Engineering Outcomes & Impact
  • ✔ ~30% Hydraulic Energy Savings: Idling pump motor speeds during direction shifts significantly reduced oil thermal wear and power.
  • ✔ Zero Chip Jams: Safety interlocking eliminated chip overflows across the machining hall floor.
  • ✔ Complete Hardware Documentation: Produced comprehensive WSCAD schematics for plant maintenance teams.
